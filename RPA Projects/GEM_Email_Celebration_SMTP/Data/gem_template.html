What you’ll see inside

• The complete treasure collection of King Tutankhamun — all 5,000+ objects from his tomb, reunited under one roof for the first time since their discovery in 1922: the golden mask, royal chariots, jewelry, furniture, and personal items that haven’t ever been displayed together in one place until now.

• A grand hall anchored by the 11-meter statue of Ramesses II, and a monumental staircase lined with colossal artifacts that literally walks you upward through thousands of years of pharaonic history.

• Immersive galleries designed with modern conservation tech, glass, and light — so the pyramids themselves are part of the museum experience, not just the view outside.

Why this matters for Egypt

This opening is not only cultural — it’s national. The museum is a multibillion-pound project years in the making, built to sit within sight of the Pyramids and to welcome leaders, guests, and families from around the world. It marks a promise: that Egypt doesn’t just protect its heritage, it presents it with the respect, scale, and confidence it deserves.

Tourism is a major pillar of our economy, and this museum is meant to help drive a new wave of cultural tourism — not just beach tourism — by making the ancient capital of human memory feel present, tangible, and proud again.
